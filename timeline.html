Smilodon Timeline

What happened to Smilodon?

These Sabertooth cats went extinct not too long ago in terms of geologic history.

Their temporal range was the Early Pleistocene to Early Holocene.

This Timeline should help you get a grasp on the timeframe of these big cats.

most recent

Acinonyx fossil, Caracal and serval fossils, and Oldest Panthera fossil

Panthera is what most people call the modern big cats like Lions, Tigers, Jaguars, Leopards, and Panthers.

2.5 Ma

The earliest species of Smilodon is S. gracilis, which existed from 2.5 million to 500,000 years ago (early Blancan to Irvingtonian ages) and was the successor in North America of Megantereon, from which it probably evolved.

1.5 Ma

Oldest Panthera tigris fossil

Modern Humans appear in Africa

11,130 BP

The most recent carbon-14 date for S. fatalis reported was 10,200 years BP for remains from the First American Cave in 1971; however, the most recent "credible" date has been given as 11,130 BP.

1839

Danish naturalist Peter Wilhelm Lund and his assistants collected fossils in the calcareous caves near the small town of Lagoa Santa, Minas Gerais, Brazil.

He identified some teeth he found as belonging to a hyena, which he named Hyaena neogaea in 1839.

After more material was found (including canine teeth and foot bones), Lund concluded the fossils instead belonged to a new Genus and species named Smilodon Populator
